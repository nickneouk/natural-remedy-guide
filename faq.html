You should always consult a qualified healthcare provider before starting any new supplement, especially if you are pregnant, nursing, taking medications, or have a medical condition. This app is designed to help you have more informed conversations with your healthcare providers, not to replace professional medical consultation.

Our information is compiled from peer-reviewed scientific literature, clinical research databases (like PubMed), government health resources (NIH, FDA, USDA), and evidence-based nutritional science. Each supplement entry includes a "last reviewed" date for transparency.

However, supplement science is constantly evolving, and individual responses vary. Always verify information with your healthcare provider and cross-check with multiple reputable sources before making health decisions.

We continuously review and update our content to reflect the latest scientific evidence and research findings. Each supplement includes a "last reviewed" date (currently January 2024). Major updates are made quarterly, while minor corrections and additions are made ongoing. We monitor new research and FDA safety announcements to keep information current.

YES, absolutely! You should always inform your healthcare providers about all supplements you are taking, including:

  • Vitamins and minerals
  • Herbal supplements
  • Probiotics and enzymes
  • Amino acids and protein supplements

Supplements can interact with medications, affect lab test results, and impact surgical procedures. Your doctor needs this information to provide safe, effective care.

If you experience a serious adverse reaction, seek immediate medical attention. Call 911 or go to the emergency room.

For non-emergency adverse reactions:

  • Stop taking the supplement immediately
  • Contact your healthcare provider
  • Report the reaction to the FDA's MedWatch program (www.fda.gov/medwatch)
  • Keep the supplement bottle for reference

The Interaction Checker helps identify potential interactions between supplements:

  1. Click "Open Interaction Checker" button on the main app page
  2. Select the supplements you're currently taking or considering (select at least 2)
  3. Click "Check Interactions"
  4. Review any identified interactions and warnings

Important: The interaction checker focuses on supplement-to-supplement interactions. Always consult your pharmacist or doctor about potential interactions with your medications.

Yes! Timing and administration can significantly impact supplement absorption and effectiveness. Each supplement detail page includes "Timing & Administration" guidance covering:

  • Best Time: Morning, evening, divided doses, or anytime
  • With Food: Required, recommended, empty stomach, or either
  • Timing Notes: Specific recommendations for optimal absorption

For example, fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) are best taken with meals containing fats, while some minerals like calcium and iron can interfere with each other and should be taken separately.
